Explore Art Movements

Baroque

Baroque art, flourishing in the 17th century, is known for its dramatic flair, grandeur, and emotional intensity. Paintings by artists like Caravaggio and Rembrandt often feature dynamic compositions, strong contrasts of light and shadow, and an emphasis on storytelling.

Impressionism

Impressionism, emerging in the late 19th century, focused on capturing fleeting moments and the effects of light and color in everyday scenes. Painters like Monet and Degas used loose brushstrokes and vibrant palettes to create works that felt immediate and atmospheric.

Renaissance

The Renaissance, spanning the 14th to 17th centuries, marked a rebirth of classical ideals in art, emphasizing realism, proportion, and perspective. Masters like Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci created iconic works that celebrated human achievement and divine beauty.

Neo-Classical

Neo-Classical art, popular in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, drew inspiration from ancient Greece and Rome, emphasizing order, harmony, and restrained emotion. Artists like Jacques-Louis David used clean lines and classical themes to convey moral and political ideals.
